Adidas

Adidas is a sports shoe company that was founded by Adolf "Adi" Dassler in Germany after World War I. He started making shoes in his mother's laundry room and later joined by his older brother Rudol. The name Adidas is an abbreviation of Adi Dassler's name. Adidas became famous when Jesse Owens wore its shoes at the 1936 Berlin Olympics.

Nike

Nike was founded in 1964 as Blue Ribbon Sports by Bill Bowerman, a track-and-field coach at the University of Oregon, and his former student Phil Knight. They opened their first retail outlet in 1966 and launched the Nike brand shoe in 1972. The company was renamed Nike, Inc., in 1978 and went public two years later. Nike successfully garnered 50 percent of the market share within the United States by 1980.

Under Armour

Under Armour, Inc. is an American sportswear company that manufactures footwear and apparel headquartered in Baltimore, Maryland, United States. Under Armour was founded on September 25, 1996, by Kevin Plank, a then-24 year old former special teams captain of the University of Maryland football team. Plank initially began the business from his grandmother's basement in Washington, D.C.

The North Face

The North Face is a company that sells outdoor equipment and clothing. It was founded in 1966 by Douglas Tompkins and his wife, Susie Tompkins, as a climbing equipment retail store in San Francisco. Two years later, it was acquired by Kenneth "Hap" Klopp. The company expanded its product line to include backpacking equipment in 1968 and outerwear in the early 1970. The company logo is inspired by Half Dome, a rock formation in Yosemite National Park.
